Section 1

Executive Summary

The Government of Andhra Pradesh stands at a singular inflection point. The Open Network for Digital Commerce (ONDC) — a protocol-based national digital commerce infrastructure mandated by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Government of India — is operational and expanding. Every transaction flowing through AP's commerce ecosystem — food delivery, grocery, ticket booking, agri produce, MSME products, GI-tagged heritage goods, and freelance services — currently generates zero direct revenue for the state exchequer. Private aggregators and national platforms extract value from AP's producers, sellers, and consumers while the state government remains a passive bystander.

Zian Technologies LLP proposes to change this architecture permanently. This document constitutes a formal proposal for the establishment of the Andhra Pradesh Commerce Stack (APCS) — a state-owned digital commerce middleware layer on ONDC — with Zian Technologies designated as both the Nodal Agency accountable to the Government of Andhra Pradesh, and the Technology Service Provider (TSP) responsible for design, development, deployment, and ongoing operations of the platform.

Background & Context

2.1 The ONDC National Framework

ONDC is an open-protocol digital commerce network initiated by DPIIT, Government of India, and operationalized under the ONDC Foundation. Unlike closed e-commerce platforms such as Amazon, Flipkart, Swiggy, or Zomato, ONDC is unbundled — any entity can become a Network Participant as a buyer app, seller app, or logistics participant. The network is live across multiple categories including food & beverages, grocery, fashion, electronics, home decor, mobility, financial services, and health. ONDC surpassed 14 lakh daily transactions by early 2024 and is growing exponentially.

The critical architecture point: ONDC is a protocol, not a platform. Whoever operates the buyer and seller application layer owns the consumer relationship, the data, and — critically — the monetization opportunity.

2.2 The Nagaland Ilandlo Precedent

In 2023, the Government of Nagaland established Ilandlo as the state-level Network Participant on ONDC — the first state government in India to assert ownership of its ONDC commerce layer. Ilandlo functions as both the buyer and seller application for Nagaland, on-boarding local producers and sellers onto the national ONDC network while retaining data sovereignty and commission revenue for the state. This precedent directly validates the governance model proposed in this document for Andhra Pradesh.

2.1 ONDC జాతీయ ఫ్రేమ్‌వర్క్

ONDC అనేది DPIIT, భారత ప్రభుత్వం ప్రారంభించిన మరియు ONDC ఫౌండేషన్ కింద నిర్వహించబడే ఓపెన్-ప్రోటోకాల్ డిజిటల్ వాణిజ్య నెట్‌వర్క్. Amazon, Flipkart, Swiggy లేదా Zomato వంటి మూసిన ఇ-కామర్స్ వేదికల వలె కాకుండా, ONDC అన్‌బండిల్ చేయబడింది — ఏ సంస్థ అయినా కొనుగోలుదారు యాప్, విక్రేత యాప్ లేదా లాజిస్టిక్స్ పాల్గొనేవారిగా నెట్‌వర్క్ పాల్గొనేవారు అవుతుంది. నెట్‌వర్క్ ఆహారం & పానీయాలు, కిరాణా, ఫ్యాషన్, ఎలక్ట్రానిక్స్, హోమ్ డెకోర్, మొబిలిటీ, ఆర్థిక సేవలు మరియు ఆరోగ్యంతో సహా అనేక వర్గాలలో నేరుగా పనిచేస్తోంది. ONDC 2024 ప్రారంభంలో రోజువారీ 14 లక్షల లావాదేవీలను దాటింది మరియు విపరీతంగా పెరుగుతోంది.

కీలకమైన నిర్మాణ అంశం: ONDC ఒక వేదిక కాదు, ఇది ఒక ప్రోటోకాల్. కొనుగోలుదారు మరియు విక్రేత దరఖాస్తు లేయర్‌ను ఎవరు నిర్వహిస్తారో వారు వినియోగదారు సంబంధాన్ని, డేటాను మరియు — ముఖ్యంగా — మోనెటైజేషన్ అవకాశాన్ని సొంతం చేసుకుంటారు.

2.2 నాగాలాండ్ Ilandlo దృష్టాంతం

2023లో, నాగాలాండ్ ప్రభుత్వం ONDC పై రాష్ట్ర-స్థాయి నెట్‌వర్క్ పాల్గొనేవారిగా Ilandlo ని స్థాపించింది — తన ONDC వాణిజ్య లేయర్ యాజమాన్యాన్ని చాటుకున్న భారతదేశంలో మొట్టమొదటి రాష్ట్ర ప్రభుత్వం. Ilandlo నాగాలాండ్‌కు కొనుగోలుదారు మరియు విక్రేత దరఖాస్తు రెండూగా పనిచేస్తుంది, స్థానిక ఉత్పత్తిదారులను మరియు విక్రేతలను జాతీయ ONDC నెట్‌వర్క్‌లో చేర్చుతూ రాష్ట్రానికి డేటా సార్వభౌమత్వం మరియు కమీషన్ ఆదాయాన్ని అందిస్తోంది. ఈ దృష్టాంతం ఈ పత్రంలో ఆంధ్రప్రదేశ్ కోసం ప్రతిపాదించిన పాలన నమూనాను నేరుగా ధృవీకరిస్తుంది.

The AP Commerce Stack — Architecture & Scope

3.1 What is APCS

The Andhra Pradesh Commerce Stack (APCS) is a state-owned digital commerce middleware platform, registered as a Network Participant on ONDC that sits between ONDC's national protocol layer and every buyer and seller in Andhra Pradesh. APCS is the technical and governance entity, through which the Government of AP asserts control, earns revenue, enforces compliance, and delivers the CM's entrepreneurship vision.

APCS operates across three integrated functional layers:

Layer 1 — Commerce Verticals
Eight commerce categories accessible to buyers and sellers: Food, Grocery, Tickets, GI Products, My Products, Services, MSME, and Agri. Each vertical is a structured module with dedicated seller on-boarding, taxonomy, compliance, and monetization rules.
Ownership / Operation
Buyer-facing apps, seller dashboards, category-specific compliance

Section 5 — Government Income Generation

Revenue Architecture

The income generation design of APCS follows the NPCI-UPI model: government-owned infrastructure where private commerce participants pay for access and premium features, while the sovereign entity retains the revenue. The following section defines each income stream, its trigger event, and its flow to the state exchequer.

INCOME PRINCIPLE

EVERY transaction on APCS is a revenue event for the Government of Andhra Pradesh. No stream is dependent on a single vertical, a single partner, or a single technology contract. Revenue scales with commerce volume — the more AP's economy grows, the more the state earns. Interests are permanently aligned.

Section 5 — Revenue Architecture

Income Stream 01 — Platform Transaction Fee

A micro-levy applied to every completed commerce transaction flowing through APCS. The rate is set by Government of AP, analogous to NPCI's MDR-equivalent for UPI commercial transactions. The fee is split-configurable: buyer-side, seller-side, or shared — with zero marginal cost to the state.

  • ▸Trigger: Order placed and delivered, or service completed on any of the 8 APCS verticals
  • ▸Collection: Deducted at settlement by APCS payment layer; remitted to GoAP on T+7 basis
  • ▸Policy lever: Differentiated rates per vertical — lower for agri and MSME to incentivize adoption; standard for food and grocery; premium for B2B

Section 5 — Revenue Architecture

Income Stream 02 — Advertising & Sponsored Listings

APCS operates a government-controlled digital advertising marketplace. Sellers, MSME manufacturers, agri processors, and national brands pay to appear at the top of APCS search results, category pages, and curated collections. The Government of AP owns the advertising inventory — analogous to Google Ads, but with the state treasury as the beneficiary.

  • ▸Sponsored Search Listings: Sellers pay per impression or per click for top-of-search placement
  • ▸Category Banner Ads: Manufacturers and brands pay for homepage and category-level display banners
  • ▸Geo-Targeted District Ads: Businesses pay premium rates to reach consumers only within their pin codes
  • ▸Seasonal Campaign Windows: Ugadi, Sankranti, Dasara, and Diwali promotional slots at premium rates — state monetizes AP's cultural calendar
  • ▸National Brand Access Fee: National brands (HUL, ITC, Marico, Reliance) pay an annual fee to reach AP's consumer base via APCS

Income Stream 03 — Brand Andhra GI Tag Certification Licensing

Manufacturers, artisan collectives, and exporters seeking to list under the authenticated Brand Andhra banner pay an annual certification fee. The state grants the certification via a designated authority; APCS administers the registry and collects the fee.

  • ▸Export certification: Exporters pay a per-shipment Brand Andhra export certification fee for international trade
  • ▸Counterfeit enforcement penalties: Sellers found misusing Brand Andhra GI certification are subject to statutory penalties — fine revenue flows to state

MSME, agri, and individual sellers pay a tiered subscription fee for access to APCS's advanced seller tools. Basic listing is free to maximize One-Entrepreneur-Per-Family enrollment.

  • ▸Free tier: Basic seller account — list products, receive orders, access standard logistics integration
  • ▸Growth tier: Analytics, priority placement, multi-category listings, automated GST filing prompts
  • ▸Enterprise tier: B2B procurement features, export module, API access, dedicated account manager

Section 5 — Revenue Architecture

Income Streams 05 & 06

Income Stream 05 — GST & Compliance Services

APCS becomes the de-facto GST compliance gateway for AP commerce. Sellers processing orders through APCS have their GST invoices auto-generated, GSTR-1 data auto-populated, and e-way bills auto-generated. This service is bundled at a per-invoice or monthly subscription fee.

  • ▸GST registration assistance: New MSME and agri sellers pay a one-time assisted registration fee
  • ▸Compliance filing subscription: Monthly fee for automated GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B filing assistance
  • ▸Policy benefit: APCS compliance data gives the state government real-time visibility into AP's informal-to-formal commerce transition

Income Stream 06 — Government Procurement Marketplace

AP government departments and local bodies can use APCS as a state-level procurement marketplace — analogous to GeM but controlled by GoAP. Vendors pay a discovery and transaction fee for government procurement orders processed through APCS.

  • ▸Vendor registration fee: One-time fee for APCS government procurement empanelment
  • ▸Bid processing fee: Per-tender or per-procurement transaction fee
  • ▸Integration with GeM: APCS government procurement module syncs with Central GeM to maximise AP MSME procurement opportunities

6.2 District-Wise Rollout Targets

  • ▸Phase 1 (Months 1–6): Pilot in 3 districts — target 10,000 entrepreneur registrations
  • ▸Phase 2 (Months 7–18): Statewide rollout across 26 districts — target 1,00,000 active entrepreneurs

  • ▸Differently-abled entrepreneurs: Dedicated on-boarding assistance; My Services includes accessibility-compatible service categories
  • ▸Rural youth (18–30): Agri, My Services, and My Products verticals; APSSDC skill certification fast-tracked
  • ▸Migrant returnee workers: Ready on-boarding to My Services and food verticals upon return to AP; APCS as re-integration tool for the AP economy

APCS as State Policy Infrastructure

Andhra Pradesh's standing on national Ease of Doing Business (EoDB) rankings is a strategic priority for the state government. APCS directly improves EoDB metrics across multiple dimensions by reducing friction, eliminating intermediaries, automating compliance, and creating transparent price discovery.

EoDB DimensionAPCS Impact
Business Registration Aadhaar-linked APCS registration auto-initiates MSME Udyam, GST, and FSSAI applications — one registration triggers multiple government processes simultaneously
Licences & Permits APCS flags required licences per seller category and auto-routes applications to relevant departments — reduces manual follow-up burden on the entrepreneur
Tax Compliance GST invoice auto-generation, GSTR-1 auto-population, e-way bill auto-generation — compliance burden reduced to near-zero for APCS sellers
Access to Markets From Day 1, the APCS seller is visible on the ONDC national network — buyers across India can transact without the seller attending a trade fair or distributor meeting

AP Commerce Stack లోని ప్రతి లావాదేవీ ఆంధ్రప్రదేశ్ ప్రభుత్వానికి ఆదాయ సంఘటన. నమోదైన ప్రతి విక్రేత శక్తివంతమైన కుటుంబం. సంపాదించిన ప్రతి రూపాయి CM యొక్క దృష్టి వాస్తవమైనది.

డిజిటల్ వాణిజ్యానికి ఓపెన్ నెట్‌వర్క్ ఆంధ్రప్రదేశ్‌కు దాని డిజిటల్ వాణిజ్య భవిష్యత్తుపై యాజమాన్యాన్ని పొందడానికి నిర్మాణాత్మక, సమయ-సున్నితమైన అవకాశాన్ని అందిస్తుంది. నాగాలాండ్ ఇలాండ్లో మోడల్ పాలన నిర్మాణాన్ని నిరూపించింది. NPCI-UPI పూర్వాపరాలు ఆదాయ మోడల్‌ను నిరూపించాయి. CM యొక్క దృష్టి సామాజిక లక్ష్యాన్ని నిర్వచించింది. మూడు థ్రెడ్‌లు AP Commerce Stack లో కలుస్తాయి.

Zian Technologies LLP అమలు చేయడానికి సిద్ధంగా ఉంది — ప్రభుత్వానికి జవాబుదారీగా ఉండే నోడల్ ఏజెన్సీగా, మరియు వేదికను అందించే టెక్నాలజీ సర్వీస్ ప్రొవైడర్‌గా. మేము ఆంధ్రప్రదేశ్ ప్రభుత్వాన్ని విభాగం 11 లో నిర్వచించిన నాలుగు దశలను ప్రారంభించమని, మరియు ఒక తరానికి AP యొక్క వాణిజ్య నిర్మాణాన్ని మార్చే భాగస్వామ్యం యొక్క ప్రారంభాన్ని అధికారం ఇవ్వమని అభ్యర్థిస్తున్నాము.
